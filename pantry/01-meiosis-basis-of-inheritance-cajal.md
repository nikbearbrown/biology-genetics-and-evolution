**Domain note:** Genetics and evolution textbook. The chromosomal theory of inheritance — Sutton's 1903 connection of Mendel's abstract factors to visible chromosome behavior during meiosis. Anaphase I as the cellular event behind Mendel's First Law; independent bivalent orientation at metaphase I behind the Second Law; linkage as the productive exception that became Sturtevant's mapping method. ## Zone Map

- **MC:** Meiosis I as the reductional division (2n → n via homolog separation). Anaphase I = Mendel's First Law in cells. Independent bivalent orientation at metaphase I = Mendel's Second Law in cells. Crossover between non-sister chromatids during prophase I → recombination frequency r → physical distance (Sturtevant's cM). X-linked inheritance asymmetry (carrier mother → half of sons affected, daughters carriers).
- **VG:** Bivalent / tetrad structure (four chromatids, paired homologs). Meiosis I vs meiosis II bookkeeping (chromosome count vs chromatid count). Sturtevant's three-gene map as a linear order recovered from pairwise distances. Pedigree iconography for X-linked-recessive disorders. Three multiplicative randomization mechanisms producing ~70 trillion zygote possibilities per couple.
- **PQ:** 46 chromosomes (2n); 23 (n). 2^23 = 8,388,608 gamete types per individual from independent assortment alone. 70 trillion zygote possibilities per couple. 50–200 crossovers per human meiosis. r = 0.20 → gamete proportions 0.40 / 0.10 / 0.10 / 0.40. 1 cM = 1% recombination frequency. Hemophilia A: ~1 in 5,000 male births. Color blindness: ~8% males, 0.5% females.
